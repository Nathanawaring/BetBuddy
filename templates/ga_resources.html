Resources and Support

If you or someone you know is struggling with gambling addiction, it's important to seek help. Below are several resources that offer confidential support, counseling, and information on managing gambling problems.

National Problem Gambling Helpline

The National Council on Problem Gambling provides free, confidential, and 24/7 helpline support across the United States. Their website also offers live chat and text services, along with information on treatment options.

Phone: 1-800-522-4700 (Available 24/7)

Website: click here

Gamblers Anonymous

Gamblers Anonymous (GA) offers a 12-step recovery program for individuals dealing with gambling problems. You can find local meetings through their website or attend virtual meetings for added convenience.

Website: click here

Gambling Therapy

Gambling Therapy provides multilingual support through its global services, including online forums, live chat, and a dedicated app. Their team offers guidance and support for people facing gambling addiction.

Website: click here

SMART Recovery

SMART Recovery is a self-help program that focuses on providing tools and support for individuals who want to overcome addictive behaviors, including gambling. The program offers free online meetings and in-person groups.

Website: click here

Gam-Anon (Support for Family & Friends)

Gam-Anon is a fellowship group for family members, friends, and loved ones of individuals with gambling problems. The program helps them find understanding, support, and coping mechanisms.

Website: click here

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

Gambling addiction can lead to severe emotional distress. If you or someone you know is experiencing suicidal thoughts, call the Suicide Prevention Lifeline for immediate help and support.

Phone: 1-800-273-8255 (Available 24/7)

Website: click here

BeGambleAware (UK)

BeGambleAware provides information, advice, and free help to anyone concerned about their gambling or someone else’s gambling in the UK. The service includes phone, chat, and online tools.

Website: click here

Self-Exclusion Programs

These programs allow individuals to exclude themselves from accessing gambling websites and apps. It’s a practical step for those who want to control their gambling habits.

United States: BetBlocker

United Kingdom: GamStop
